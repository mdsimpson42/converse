There are a number of resources available to provide information and support for a range of mental health issues.

Emergency Support

If you are in immediate danger, call 999 or go to your nearest Accident and Emergency department.

You can also:

  • Call Samaritans on 116 123 (UK-wide)
  • Text SHOUT to 85258 (UK-wide)
  • Call The C.A.L.L. Helpline on 0800 132 737 (Wales)

If you are not in immediate danger, but still need urgent advice or support call 111 or speak to your GP.

More Information and Support

There are many organisations that can provide information and support for mental health issues. Below is a list of some of the main organisations in the UK.

Organisation Services
Mind Mind have a range of information, including an A-Z of Mental Health, as well as access to tools and services for people in England and Wales.
SAMH Scottish Association for Mental Health (SAMH) provides information and support for people in Scotland.
Action Mental Health Action Mental Health is a leading charity transforming lives and promoting mental wellbeing, with support groups all across Northern Ireland.
NHS The National Health Service (NHS) provides a range of mental health services in mainland UK. Or Health and Social Care Northern Ireland (HSC) in NI.
CALM The Campaign Against Living Miserably (CALM) is a suicide provention charity that provides information and hosts a webchat service and a private online forum.
MindOut MindOut is an organisation that provides specialised services run by and for LGBTQ+ people.
Scope Scope provides a range of mental health information tailored for disabled people.
Hub of Hope Hub of Hope allows you to search for local, national, peer, community, charity, private and NHS mental health support.

Note: Apologies that the information on this page is very UK-centric. International users should seek out local resources and support services. If you would like help us by updating this page with information for your country, please get in touch via the Contribute page.
